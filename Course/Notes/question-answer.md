# Comprehensive Data Visualization MCQ Quiz
*Topics Covered: Information Visualization Basics, Gestalt Principles, Exploratory Data Analysis, Chart Types, Data Scaling, and Tamara Munzner's Framework.*

**Q1. According to Gestalt principles, which principle states that elements physically connected to each other are perceived as part of the same group?**
- A) Principle of Proximity
- B) Principle of Similarity
- C) Principle of Connection
- D) Principle of Continuity

**Answer:** C) Principle of Connection

---

**Q2. [Multi-Correct] Which of the following are considered primary Design Elements in Data Visualization?**
- A) Line
- B) Shape
- C) Gestalt
- D) Texture

**Answer:** A, B, D

---

**Q3. If a dataset's distribution has a flatter peak and thinner tails compared to a normal distribution, what type of kurtosis does it exhibit?**
- A) Mesokurtic
- B) Leptokurtic
- C) Platykurtic
- D) Negative Skewness

**Answer:** C) Platykurtic

---

**Q4. What is the primary lesson learned from Anscombe's Quartet?**
- A) Datasets with identical summary statistics will always look identical when graphed.
- B) Summary statistics are sufficient to understand data without visualization.
- C) Datasets with identical descriptive statistics can have vastly different distributions and visual representations.
- D) Correlation always implies causation in small datasets.

**Answer:** C) Datasets with identical descriptive statistics can have vastly different distributions and visual representations.

---

**Q5. According to Ben Shneiderman's Visual Information Seeking Mantra, what sequence of actions should a user follow?**
- A) Overview first, details-on-demand, then zoom and filter
- B) Details-on-demand, zoom and filter, overview first
- C) Overview first, zoom and filter, then details-on-demand
- D) Zoom and filter, overview first, details-on-demand

**Answer:** C) Overview first, zoom and filter, then details-on-demand

---

**Q6. Which chart is BEST suited to visualize the flow of resources or quantities between multiple stages, where the width of the arrows is proportional to the flow quantity?**
- A) Waterfall Chart
- B) Sankey Diagram
- C) Marimekko Chart
- D) Chord Diagram

**Answer:** B) Sankey Diagram

---

**Q7. [Multi-Correct] Which of the following statements are TRUE regarding Marimekko charts?**
- A) It visualizes categorical data over two numeric variables.
- B) Both the X and Y axes correspond to a percentage scale (0-100%).
- C) It is ideal for identifying trends over long periods of time.
- D) The area of each rectangle is proportional to the overall data value.

**Answer:** A, B, D

---

**Q8. A chart used specifically to show how an initial value is affected by a series of intermediate positive or negative values, leading to a final value, is called a:**
- A) Waterfall Chart
- B) Diverging Bar Chart
- C) Streamgraph
- D) Bump Chart

**Answer:** A) Waterfall Chart

---

**Q9. Who introduced the concept of "Sparklines", describing them as "data-intense, design-simple, word-sized graphics"?**
- A) Ben Shneiderman
- B) Tamara Munzner
- C) Edward Tufte
- D) John Tukey

**Answer:** C) Edward Tufte

---

**Q10. How does a Connected Scatterplot differ from a standard Scatterplot or Line Chart?**
- A) It plots a single variable across a continuous time axis.
- B) It plots two variables simultaneously on the X and Y axes, using a line to connect the points in chronological order.
- C) It removes the X and Y axes entirely to save space.
- D) It connects categorical data points to show a hierarchical structure.

**Answer:** B) It plots two variables simultaneously on the X and Y axes, using a line to connect the points in chronological order.

---

**Q11. Which chart type is essentially a variation of a stacked area chart that displaces around a central axis (resembling a flowing river), commonly used to show volume changes over time without relying on a fixed straight axis?**
- A) Horizon Chart
- B) Streamgraph
- C) Cycle Chart
- D) Waffle Chart

**Answer:** B) Streamgraph

---

**Q12. [Multi-Correct] In the context of Big Data and Visual Analytics, which of the following are part of the standard "V"s?**
- A) Volume
- B) Velocity
- C) Vector
- D) Variety

**Answer:** A, B, D (along with Veracity)

---

**Q13. In Tamara Munzner's Nested Model of Visualization Design, which level involves translating domain-specific questions into a generalized vocabulary of visualization?**
- A) Domain Situation
- B) Abstraction (Data and Task)
- C) Idiom (Visual Encoding and Interaction)
- D) Algorithm

**Answer:** B) Abstraction (Data and Task)

---

**Q14. In Munzner's classification of "Search" actions, if a user knows EXACTLY what they are looking for (Target is known) and EXACTLY where to find it (Location is known), what search action are they performing?**
- A) Locate
- B) Explore
- C) Browse
- D) Lookup

**Answer:** D) Lookup

---

**Q15. [Multi-Correct] Based on Munzner's framework, which of the following are considered "Marks" (geometric primitives) in visual encoding?**
- A) Points
- B) Lines
- C) Color
- D) Areas

**Answer:** A, B, D (Color is a Channel, not a Mark)

---

**Q16. According to the effectiveness ranking of visual channels, which channel is generally considered the most accurate and effective for encoding quantitative (ordered/numerical) data?**
- A) Color Hue
- B) Spatial Position
- C) Area (Size)
- D) Shape

**Answer:** B) Spatial Position

---

**Q17. The analytical process of dealing with missing numerical data by replacing blank values with the mean or median of that feature is known as:**
- A) Categorical Encoding
- B) Feature Splitting
- C) Numerical Imputation
- D) Discretization

**Answer:** C) Numerical Imputation

---

**Q18. [Multi-Correct] Which of the following are techniques used in Data Scaling?**
- A) Min-Max Normalization
- B) One-Hot Encoding
- C) Z-score Standardization
- D) Decimal Scaling

**Answer:** A, C, D (One-Hot Encoding is for categorical data, not scaling)

---

**Q19. When working with Machine Learning models that require features to be centered around zero with a standard deviation of 1, which scaling method is most appropriate?**
- A) Absolute Maximum Scaling
- B) Standardization (Z-score)
- C) Discretization
- D) Min-Max Scaling

**Answer:** B) Standardization (Z-score)

---

**Q20. [Multi-Correct] What are the primary differences between Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Confirmatory Data Analysis (CDA)?**
- A) EDA focuses on discovering new patterns and formulating hypotheses without strict preconceptions.
- B) CDA uses rigorous statistical tests to confirm or reject previously formulated hypotheses.
- C) EDA relies heavily on visual techniques and numerical summaries rather than p-values.
- D) CDA is primarily used to clean raw data before exploration begins.

**Answer:** A, B, C
